# **Dynamic Geometry Standard Model (DGSM) — v1.0**

### *A Unified Structural Framework for Dynamic Systems*

---

# **0. Overview**

The **Dynamic Geometry Standard Model (DGSM)** provides a unified mathematical framework for describing, analyzing, and predicting the behavior of dynamic systems. DGSM treats every dynamic process as a transformation within a structured geometric space shaped by rule constraints, feedback interactions, and stability conditions.

DGSM integrates three foundational layers:

1. **Rule Layer** — defines admissible transformations and generative constraints.
2. **Dynamic Layer** — governs co-evolving states and structural configurations.
3. **Geometry Layer** — captures stability, attractor structure, and the projection of deeper dynamics into observable behavior.

DGSM formalizes dynamic systems not as isolated functions or differential equations, but as **geometric processes driven by structured rule interactions**, enabling a unified treatment of continuous, discrete, stochastic, and hybrid systems.

---

# **1. Motivation**

Traditional dynamic system theory lacks a unifying principle that integrates:

* Generative rules
* Structural evolution
* Stability geometry
* Multi-attractor behavior
* System-wide transformation constraints

DGSM addresses these gaps by defining a canonical structure in which all dynamic systems can be represented.

This framework is:

* **Domain-agnostic** — applicable to physics, biology, computation, and artificial systems.
* **Scale-invariant** — compatible with micro-, meso-, and macro-scale processes.
* **Predictive** — stability and evolution paths arise from geometric constraints.
* **Extensible** — supports multi-layer, multi-rule, or evolving-rule systems.

---

# **2. The Three-Layer Structure**

## **2.1 Rule Layer (Generative Structure)**

The Rule Layer defines the space of admissible transformations. It encodes:

* Interaction rules
* Constraints and symmetries
* Allowable transitions
* Degrees of freedom

This layer acts as the *source* of all possible system behaviors. DGSM does not require explicit enumeration of rules—only that a generative structure exists.

### **Rule Layer Core Postulates:**

1. Every dynamic system arises from a generative structure.
2. Rules determine the allowable geometry of state transitions.
3. Rule-consistency is required for stable dynamics.

---

## **2.2 Dynamic Layer (State–Structure Co-evolution)**

Dynamic systems are represented as co-evolving processes where:

* States and structures evolve together.
* Feedback mechanisms influence both state change and structure rearrangement.
* Dynamics are inseparable from structural constraints.

This replaces the classical assumption that dynamics operate on a fixed structure.

### **Dynamic Layer Core Postulates:**

1. State and structure form a unified evolving pair.
2. Feedback loops are intrinsic and must be preserved.
3. Dynamics follow paths permitted by the Rule Layer.

---

## **2.3 Geometry Layer (Stability & Attractor Geometry)**

The Geometry Layer captures the global behavior of dynamic systems via:

* Attractor basins
* Stability manifolds
* Transition geometry
* Multi-basin interactions

The geometry provides the **observable shape** of deeper rule-driven dynamics.

### **Geometry Layer Core Postulates:**

1. Stability is a geometric property.
2. Attractors represent long-term system outcomes.
3. Geometric constraints explain universal dynamic patterns.

---

# **3. DGSM Axioms**

DGSM is defined by the following minimal axiom set:

### **Axiom 1 — Generativity**

Every dynamic system is generated by a rule-consistent structure.

### **Axiom 2 — Co-Evolution**

State and structure evolve jointly under rule constraints.

### **Axiom 3 — Continuity of Transformation**

Dynamic evolution follows rule-admissible geometric paths.

### **Axiom 4 — Stability Geometry**

Stable behaviors correspond to geometric basins in the system's state–structure space.

### **Axiom 5 — Invariance Under Projection**

Observable dynamics are projections of deeper geometric processes.

---

# **4. Dynamic Evolution & Stability (DEST) Integration**

DGSM incorporates DEST as its operational engine:

* Systems evolve toward deeper stability basins.
* Geometry restricts possible evolution paths.
* Stability transitions occur at geometric boundaries.
* Multistability arises naturally from rule-geometry interplay.

DEST provides:

* Unified treatment of bifurcations
* Explanation for spontaneous pattern formation
* Predictive rules for dynamic transitions

---

# **5. System Classification Under DGSM**

DGSM categorizes dynamic systems into:

### **Class I — Fixed-rule dynamics**

Rules remain constant.

* Classical ODE systems
* Discrete maps
* Reaction networks

### **Class II — Co-evolving structure dynamics**

Structure evolves with state.

* Adaptive networks
* Feedback-driven processes

### **Class III — Evolving-rule dynamics**

Rules can change over time.

* Evolutionary systems
* Developmental processes
* Self-organizing computational systems

DGSM fully supports all three classes.

# **7. Standard Compliance Requirements**

A dynamic model is DGSM-compliant if it:

1. Identifies its generative rule structure.
2. Specifies coupled evolution of states and structure.
3. Defines a stability geometry or attractor structure.
4. Ensures projection consistency across layers.
